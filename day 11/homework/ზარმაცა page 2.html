Sloths (lat. Bradypodidae) — a family of mammals of the order of incomplete teeth. Their body length is 50-64 cm, weight 4-7 kg. The front legs are longer than the back. The fingers end in cell-like claws. Unlike other mammals, sloths have 6-9 neck vertebrae and 14-24 pairs of ribs. The body is covered with grayish-brown fur and often has a greenish tinge because the fur is inhabited by microscopic blue-green algae. The sloth has a well-developed sense of smell and vision, less so hearing. They always hang with their backs down on the trees, which is why their internal organs are unusually arranged for mammals: the liver is turned towards the back and does not touch the abdominal wall; The spleen and pancreas are on the right, the urinary bladder is large and almost touches the diaphragm, the trachea forms two coils, etc. There are 7 species in the family, which are united in 2 genera: three-toed sloths (Bradypus) and two-toed sloths (Choloepus). They are common in the forests of South and Central America. They live as individuals, rarely in pairs. They give birth to one baby once a year. They feed on leaves, twigs and fruits.
